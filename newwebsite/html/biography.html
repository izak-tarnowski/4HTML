Biography

Tupac Amaru Shakur, born Lesane Parish Crooks on June 16, 1971 in New York City, after one year was renamed to Tupac Amaru II, after an 18th century Peruvian revolutionist against the Spanish Rule. He grew up in East Harlem, of Manhatten in New York City. His parents, Afeni Shakur and Billy Garland were active members of the Black Panther Party in New York, NY during the 60s and early 70s. Tupac was born a month after his mother was acquitted of more than 150 chargesof "Conspiracy against the US Government and New York landmarks" in the Panther 21 trial. He basically grew up around long-time criminals, some being extremely serious crimes eg murder, aggravated robbery. His step-father even spent 4 years on the FBI's Most Wanted Fugitive List. He also had a step-brother, Mopreme "Komani" Shakur, who performed with Tupac on a few of his recordings.









During his stardom, Tupac had met many, many other famous icons, for example Tupac met Kiss, the rock band, he befriended Mike Tyson, the world famous boxer and became "Amerikaz Most Wanted" with Snoop Dogg, he even dated Madonna for some part of 1993. Tupac has worked with many, many different names, including;
  1. Dr. Dre
  2. Snoop Dogg
  3. The Notorious BIG (Biggie Smalls)
  4. Dramacydal (group)
  5. Nas
  6. Bone Thugs n Harmony (group)
  7. the Outlawz (group)
Tupac Shakur was murdered in a drive-by shooting on the night of September 7th, 1996, on East Flamingo Road and Koval Lane in Las Vegas, NV. He attended the Bruce Seldon vs. Mike Tyson boxing match at the MGM Grand with his entourage and Suge Knight. They spotted a rival gang member, who allegedly robbed a member of their record label and Tupac attacked him with his entourage. This lead to the rival member, Orlando Anderson or "Baby Lane", to be a prime suspect in the murder of Tupac. They left the MGM Grand and made their way to a club that Suge Knight owned. On the way, they were stopped at traffic lights where a white Cadillac pulled along side and started firing at the car that Tupac was in. He was hit 4 times, while Suge was only hit with fragmentation on the head. 6 days later, on September 13th 1996, Tupac was pronounced dead from the wounds he received during the shooting. Lots of high-ranked rappers, including Biggie Smalls (before his death in 1997), Snoop Dogg, Eminem, the Outlawz and Bone Thugs n Harmony all mourned Tupac. Multiple interviews with Biggie Smalls gave insight to how he felt about Tupac, despite their problems, they were always close.
